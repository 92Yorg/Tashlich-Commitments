Tashlich

  • During the Rosh Hashanah holiday, many individuals and synagogues observe a fun outdoor tradition that originated in the Middle Ages. It’s called Tashlich (pronounced tash-leekh), which is the Hebrew word for “casting off / throwing off.”

    People gather together at a body of flowing water—often a nearby river, but it can be a lake or even the ocean—and they bring something to throw in the water (often breadcrumbs are used however pebbles, leaves or even peas are better for the earth and creatures inside the water). To start, take a handful of what you’ve chosen to toss and imagine that they represent all of our misdeeds over the course of the past year. Then toss them into the water, symbolically “casting our sins upon the waters.”

  • Tashlich allows us to physically cast out our sins during the Teshuvah (repentance) process of self-reflection, of asking for forgiveness and of offering forgiveness.
